# Product Brief — Identity Trust & Inclusion Fabric

## Product thesis

Organizations already possess matchers, document checks, liveness engines, registries, wallets, rules, and case-management tools. What is usually missing is a vendor-neutral and rights-preserving layer that can combine their evidence, detect both fraud and exclusion, and select the least intrusive effective action.

## Customer problem

The architectural box called **Fraud Detection** frequently has no agreed fraud ontology, evidence contract, decision authority, review workflow, ground truth, or measurable service objective. This ambiguity creates four risks:

1. commodity controls are mistaken for end-to-end fraud management;
2. rare or novel fraud is invisible to supervised rules;
3. false suspicion propagates without repair;
4. legitimate people are silently excluded by long, repeated, or inaccessible processes.

## Product promise

The Fabric provides a purpose-bound assurance decision for a specific transaction or credential presentation. It does **not** compute a universal person score.

### Inputs

- face, fingerprint, and optional iris scores and quality;
- PAD, injection, document, and issuer-trust signals;
- verifiable-credential status and holder-binding evidence;
- device, network, behavioral, and transaction metadata;
- process attempts, duration, abandonment, and alternative-path evidence;
- privacy-preserving relationship indicators.

### Outputs

- calibrated risk and novelty scores;
- evidence gaps and alternative hypotheses;
- exclusion-risk indicators;
- recommended action and reason codes;
- immutable decision, policy, and model provenance;
- correction and redress propagation state.

## Differentiation

- government-grade and sovereign deployment;
- fingerprint-first as well as face-capable;
- vendor-neutral post-matcher integration;
- graph and credential assurance beyond one transaction;
- anti-exclusion objective co-equal with anti-fraud;
- deterministic remedy and record repair;
- privacy-preserving federation rather than raw-data centralization;
- shadow-mode adoption and evidence-based production gates.

## Product modules

1. **Evidence Gateway** — schemas, provenance, quality, and policy checks.
2. **Trust Graph** — issuer, credential, holder, verifier, device, and event relationships.
3. **Risk Lab** — rules, supervised models, anomaly detection, GNN, calibration, and ablations.
4. **Inclusion Observatory** — repeated failure, delay, abandonment, channel and site anomalies.
5. **Action Orchestrator** — approve, monitor, step-up, human review, block/escalate.
6. **Remedy Engine** — appeal, correction, reprocessing, and propagation verification.
7. **Evidence Cockpit** — investigator, operator, citizen, auditor, and executive views.

## Initial beachhead

Start with a single high-value identity process in shadow mode. Integrate score-level evidence and process telemetry, detect disagreement and exclusion patterns, and measure usefulness at a fixed review capacity. Do not automate adverse decisions during the pilot.

## Commercial model options

- annual sovereign platform license;
- per-transaction assurance API for private-sector customers;
- managed model calibration and threat-intelligence subscription;
- integration accelerators for ABIS, wallets, telcos, and credential issuers;
- independent assurance and benchmark service.

## Go/no-go gates

A production investment requires repeatable demand across customers, lawful data access, defined fraud and exclusion outcomes, review capacity, feedback labels, measurable economic value, and a deployment model compatible with sovereignty and privacy requirements.
