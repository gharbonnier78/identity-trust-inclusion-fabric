## Observed whitespace

The proposed Fabric does not claim an empty market. Its opportunity is a combination that is less commonly productized as one governed layer:

- post-matcher evidence coherence across fingerprint, face, and optional iris;
- credential, issuer, holder, verifier, device, and process graph;
- on-premise or sovereign federation;
- vendor-neutral integration of existing biometric components;
- open-set anomaly detection alongside known-fraud classification;
- anti-exclusion monitoring as a first-class product outcome;
- citizen remedy and downstream record repair;
- explicit optimization of risk, friction, review capacity, privacy, and delay.

## Build, buy, partner

Buy or partner for commodity matching, PAD, document authenticity, device intelligence, sanctions screening, and wallet plumbing. Build the evidence ontology, calibration, anomaly and graph fusion, inclusion observatory, action policy, remedy, and traceability layer.

## Competitive validation questions

1. Can the offer run fully on-premise and across sovereign domains?
2. Does it support fingerprint evidence beyond face onboarding?
3. Can customers bring multiple matcher and credential vendors?
4. Does it distinguish anomaly, suspicion, confirmed fraud, and administrative error?
5. Does it detect and remediate exclusion patterns?
6. Can an adverse decision be appealed and repaired across relying parties?
7. Are thresholds calibrated to review capacity and customer-specific costs?
